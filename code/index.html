Kungliga Slottet, or the Royal Palace, is one of Stockholm's most popular tourist attractions. It is the official residence of the Swedish royal family and is located in the heart of the city. The palace is open to the public for tours, and there are a number of family-friendly activities available. Kungliga Slottet is a great place to spend a day with the family. There is something for everyone to enjoy, and you can learn about Swedish history and culture while you're there.

Junibacken is a children's museum in Stockholm, Sweden, dedicated to children's literature. It is located on the island of Djurgården, and is home to a number of interactive exhibits based on popular Swedish children's books, such as Astrid Lindgren's Pippi Longstocking and The Moomins. Junibacken is a great place for children to let their imaginations run wild. They can dress up as their favorite characters, play in the toy houses,and will love the opportunity to interact with their favorite characters.

Museums

Located in the heart of Stockholm, Naturhistoriska riksmuseet, or the Swedish Museum of Natural History, is a great place for family tourists to learn about the natural world. The museum has a wide variety of exhibits, including dinosaurs, animals, plants, and minerals. There are also interactive exhibits that kids will love, such as the dinosaur dig and the butterfly house. It is easily accessible by public transportation and is open every day, and admission is free for children under 18.

Tom Tits Experiment is a science center in Stockholm, Sweden, where you can learn about science through interactive experiments. With over 400 different experiments, there's something for everyone at Tom Tits Experiment, from kids to adults. The experiments are all hands-on, so you can get involved and learn by doing. There are experiments on everything from physics to biology to psychology. You can even try your hand at flying a plane or building a robot.

Sjöhistoriska museet, or the Swedish Maritime Museum, is a great place for family tourists to learn about the history of Swedish shipping and seafaring. The museum has a wide variety of exhibits, including everything from model ships to submarine rides. There are also interactive exhibits that kids will love, such as the Tug of War exhibit, which lets kids experience the power of tugboats or the Seat of Power exhibit, which lets kids explore the bridge of a modern warship.

Fyrishov Waterpark is a great place to cool off on a hot summer day. With over 25 different water slides, a wave pool, and a lazy river, there's something for everyone at Fyrishov Waterpark. The water park is located in Uppsala, just a short train ride from Stockholm.

Husbybadet is a great place for families to cool off during summer time. There are three different pools, including a main pool with a diving board, a children's pool with a slide, and a wave pool. There is also a sauna, a solarium, and a playground. For those who want to get some sun, there's a large grassy area with plenty of space to spread out. There are also picnic tables and barbecue grills available for those who want to bring their own food.

Kristinebergs slottspark is a beautiful park in Stockholm that was once the grounds of a royal castle. 4H-petfarms

Stora Skuggans 4H-gård is a city farm located in Norra Djurgården in Stockholm. The farm has a variety of animals, including cows, pigs, sheep, goats, chickens, ducks, rabbits, and guinea pigs. There is also a vegetable garden, a playground, and a forest area. Visitors can learn about the animals on the farm and how to care for them through educational programs for children on topics such as animal care, gardening, and environmental sustainability.
